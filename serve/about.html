About Me

My name is David A. Meyer. I am 38 years old. I was born in New Jersey, grew up in Western New York and have lived in Central Florida since 2001 with my best friend and wife, Micki. We recently had our first child, James, who has turned our lives upside-down in the best ways imaginable.

For the past 11 years, I have worked in marketing for a global commercial real estate firm. Before that, I was a sportswriter and editor for Messenger-Post Newspapers in Rochester, New York. Other jobs included, but were not limited to:

  • Patching roads, flagging traffic and retrieving road kill
  • Mass-producing bolts, nuts and screws
  • Making boxes
  • Washing dishes (Why yes, I am certified in New York State.)
  • Bar-backing
  • Slinging sandwiches, cold cuts and produce for Danny like every other upstanding upstate teen

Growing up, I wanted nothing more than to be a professional baseball player. Needless to say, that did not happen. I made it as far as a low-level Division I program in northern Indiana, but mostly in the role of mascot.

I bounced around a bit and eventually received degrees in Communications and Liberal Arts from Monroe Community College before earning a B.S. in Communications with a Political Science minor at the State University of New York (SUNY) College at Fredonia.

I love to read, watch movies and enjoy good television -- when I can find it. Technology fascinates me. Politics simultaneously intrigue and disgust me. Photography remains a passion despite dwindling time and resources. Personal fitness and diet have become central to my life since learning I would be a father at a relatively advanced age (I would like to stick around).

Finally, I am a die-hard Pittsburgh sports fan. The Steelers, Pirates, Penguins and University of Pittsburgh have been my unhealthy obsession for the past 30 years.

About the Site

This site was built using Jekyll. I cannot take credit for the site's design. I'm using the default layout. I hope to create a custom theme in the near future, but this is pretty great right off the shelf. Light, simple and functional with only a few minor quibbles.

When writing for this site, I usually do so on an Apple 15" MacBook Pro or 24" iMac. Both are dated pieces of hardware, but continue to function superbly. I write in Markdown and my editor du jour is BBEdit.

I recently purchased a used Lenovo X201 on which I'm running Ubuntu. This combination has its limitations, but I'm having a hoot experimenting with Free and Open Source Software. And frankly, for writing, it exceeds my needs.
