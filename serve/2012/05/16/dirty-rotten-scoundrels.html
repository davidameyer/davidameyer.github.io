Dirty Rotten Scoundrels

16 May 2012

Matt Taibbi writes in Rolling Stone about a legal gaffe by an attorney for Goldman Sachs and Bank of America/Merrill Lynch which revealed a treasure trove of formerly sealed documents detailing just how complicit these firms were in the practice of naked short selling.

The import of this is that it made it cheaper and easier to bet down the value of a stock, while simultaneously devaluing the same stock by adding fake supply. This makes it easier to make money by destroying value, and is another example of how the over-financialization of the economy makes real, job-creating growth more difficult.

In short, banks were fabricating value where there was none. Sound Familiar?
